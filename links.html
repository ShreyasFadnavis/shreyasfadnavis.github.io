How I Ended Up Here

My PhD advisor Eleftherios Garyfallidis (creator of DIPY) handed me a problem about noise in diffusion MRI. Studying noise means studying variance, which means studying leverage, which led to conformal prediction. The path was: denoising → residual variance → leverage → conformal prediction.

Leverage Scores & Randomized Linear Algebra

Most of what I know about leverage scores, I learned from Petros Drineas. I started working with him during my PhD, and he opened up an entire world of randomized numerical linear algebra that I didn't know existed. Petros is the Head of CS at Purdue and a SIAM Fellow. His work on fast approximation of statistical leverage — the idea that you can sketch a matrix and still know which rows matter — is foundational.

A lot of the leverage scores literature I absorbed came through working alongside Agniva Chowdhury, who was Petros's PhD student at Purdue and is now an AI Research Scientist at Intel.
