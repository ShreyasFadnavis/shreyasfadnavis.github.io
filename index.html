About Writing

Shreyas Fadnavis

Machine learning, applied math, and the geometry of uncertainty.

I am a researcher working at the intersection of statistical learning, computer vision, and biomedical engineering. Currently a postdoctoral research scientist at Johnson & Johnson, where I develop foundational models for medical imaging (echocardiography, endoscopy) and work on interpretability methods for deep learning.

Previously, I was a postdoctoral fellow at Harvard Medical School, where I worked on multimodal machine learning, diffusion MRI, and computational psychiatry. I hold a PhD from Indiana University Bloomington in Engineering (Intelligent Systems), where I created Patch2Self—a self-supervised framework for denoising diffusion MRI data, presented as a spotlight at NeurIPS 2020.

My current research interests include conformal prediction, leverage scores, randomized numerical linear algebra, and uncertainty quantification.
